# Roles and access

This page explains who can see what, how roles are assigned, and how the correction request flow works.

---

## The three roles

### Team member
The default role for everyone on the Nigeria team.

**Can see:** Home, team pulse, relationships, leave tracker, projects, Slack intel, visits.

**Can do:**
- View all information on their accessible tabs
- Update their own personal status (WFH, unavailable this afternoon) — no approval needed
- Submit correction requests for any data that looks wrong

**Cannot do:**
- Edit any shared data directly
- See the opportunities, funding analysis, or global pipeline tabs
- Access the admin panel

---

### Partnerships lead
Everything the team member can see, plus the strategy modules.

**Can see:** All of the above, plus opportunities, funding analysis, and global pipeline.

**Can do:**
- Everything a team member can do
- Edit opportunities and pipeline data in the admin panel

---

### Country director
Full access to everything.

**Can see:** All ten tabs.

**Can do:**
- Everything the partnerships lead can do
- Review and action correction requests from team members
- Access the admin panel (all sections)
- Manage user roles
- Approve or reject team member correction requests

---

## How roles are assigned

Roles are managed in the admin panel under **Users**. Only the country director and superadmin can change roles.

To change a team member's role:
1. Go to the admin panel (requires login)
2. Click **Users**
3. Find the person's name
4. Change their role from the dropdown
5. Click Save

The change takes effect the next time that person logs in. If they are already logged in, they need to log out and back in.

## The correction request flow

Team members cannot edit shared data directly. This is by design — it keeps the data consistent and avoids accidental changes. Instead, team members submit a correction request.

### How it works

**Step 1:** The team member sees something incorrect — for example, their expertise tag shows "health" when it should be "energy".

**Step 2:** They click the "Request a correction" button on that data card. A panel slides up asking:
- What field needs correcting?
- What does it currently show?
- What should it show?
- Any additional context?

**Step 3:** The request is logged with their name, timestamp, and details. The country director sees a badge notification in the admin panel.

**Step 4:** The country director reviews the request in the admin panel under **Pending corrections**. They can:
- Approve it (the data updates immediately)
- Reject it with a note explaining why

**Step 5:** The team member sees the outcome — approved or rejected, with the director's note — the next time they open the dashboard.

### Personal status is different

Your personal status ("Working from home today", "Unavailable until 3pm") is separate from shared data. You can update this yourself at any time without going through the correction request flow. It appears as a small overlay on the schedule view and does not require approval.

## What the restricted screen means

If you navigate to a tab and see a padlock icon with the message "Access restricted", it means that tab requires a higher role than your current one.

This is not an error — the tab is working correctly. If you believe you should have access (for example, you have recently become the partnerships lead), ask the country director to update your role in the admin panel.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I see my own correction requests?**
Yes. Once submitted, your requests appear in the Slack intel tab under "My requests" with their current status (pending, approved, or rejected with a note).

**What if I need to update something urgently and can't wait for the director to review?**
Contact the country director directly — they can update the data immediately from the admin panel without going through the correction request queue.

**Can I submit a correction request for someone else's data?**
Yes, if you notice an error in another team member's record (for example, a colleague is listed on the wrong project), you can submit a correction request on their behalf. Note their name in the "additional context" field.

**Who can see correction requests?**
Only the person who submitted them (in their Slack intel view) and the country director and superadmin (in the admin panel).
